Which Model?

Choosing the Right Vertix Turbine

Commercial or Industrial Load

GV-3KW or GVH-5KW

If you are running a factory, warehouse or commercial building with a significant electricity load and want wind to contribute meaningfully to your hybrid system, the GV range is the correct choice. Both are IEC certified, both require a structural assessment for rooftop installation, and both integrate into solar + BESS hybrid architectures. Choose GVH-5KW for larger loads and sites that can accommodate a 10 m tower and 670 kg turbine.

Open Site, Consistent Wind Direction

M-Type

If your site is in an open rural or coastal location with consistent prevailing wind direction, and you need 300–500W for off-grid or supplementary charging at 12/24V, the M-Type is the right choice. It is a horizontal-axis turbine — it requires directional wind and is not suited to turbulent rooftop environments or urban settings. Best for farmland, coastal installations and rural industrial facilities.

Off-Grid, Remote or Compact

Q-Type or H-Type

For remote monitoring stations, telecoms, security installations or any application where a compact, low-output, low-maintenance wind turbine is needed alongside a 12/24V battery bank, the Q-Type or H-Type is appropriate. The Q-Type has higher output (100–400W) and better survival wind (45 m/s). The H-Type is the most compact and generates at the lowest wind speeds (2 m/s) but has a lower survival rating of 35 m/s.
