The HED Methodology

Hybrid Engineering & Design — What It Actually Means

HED is not an engineering firm. It is the way Charania Renewables approaches every project. Most energy system failures in Pakistan are not product failures — they are design failures. Systems sized by guesswork, economics modelled on optimistic assumptions, technology selected because a salesperson pushed it. HED is the counter to all of that.

We start with your consumption data — utility bills, smart meter readings, production schedules, critical load lists. We model the economics: what does grid energy actually cost you today, what would a hybrid system save, what is the payback period and internal rate of return at conservative assumptions. Only once the financial case is clear do we specify technology. The system design follows the numbers. The numbers come from your operation.

Engineering and installation are delivered through our EPC and installation partners. CR sits as design authority and project coordinator — one point of accountability from brief to handover.

Who We Work With

Built for Commercial and Industrial Pakistan

Our solutions focus is industrial and commercial — factories, warehouses, hospitals, cold chain facilities, large commercial buildings and institutions. These are the clients where energy cost is a significant line item, where grid unreliability has a quantifiable production cost, and where a well-designed hybrid system delivers a return that justifies the capital investment.

We also work with EPC partners who need a design and financial modelling capability behind their project bids. If you are an EPC tendering on a hybrid energy project and need engineering design, system architecture, SLDs and financial modelling, the HED methodology is available to support your bid. We do not compete with EPCs on installation — we work alongside them as the design and advisory layer.

Beyond the Capital Purchase

Not every client wants to own the system. For commercial and industrial clients where capital allocation is constrained, Charania Renewables is developing Energy as a Service (EaaS) structures — where the hybrid system is financed, installed and operated by CR or a financial partner, and the client pays for the energy delivered rather than the hardware.
