Hybrid Generation

Solar alone generates nothing after sunset or on overcast days. Wind alone is idle when the air is still. Combined — with battery storage behind them — they produce power across more hours of the day, in more weather conditions, than either source can achieve independently. Charania Renewables integrates Vertix vertical-axis wind and Tier-1 solar PV into hybrid generation systems designed for Pakistan’s industrial and commercial sector.

The Generation Problem

Why Solar Alone Is Not Enough

Pakistan’s industrial sector operates 24 hours a day. Solar generation peaks between 10am and 4pm and produces nothing at night. During Pakistan’s monsoon season and on overcast winter days, generation drops significantly even during daylight hours. A solar-only system designed to meet full daytime load will still require full grid draw at night and partial grid draw on poor-irradiance days. The economics of solar-only are good but not transformative for a continuous-operation facility.

Adding wind generation through Vertix changes this equation. Wind generates when solar does not — at night, in overcast conditions and during early morning and evening hours. The two sources have naturally complementary generation profiles. Combined with battery storage to smooth supply curves and absorb excess generation, a hybrid wind-solar-BESS system delivers a higher proportion of on-site generation than solar alone — and a more bankable energy cost projection over a 10–15 year horizon.

Vertix — CR Wind Brand

Vertical-Axis Wind for Urban and Industrial Pakistan

Vertix is Charania Renewables’ own wind generation brand. Conventional horizontal-axis turbines require large open sites with consistent prevailing wind direction — conditions rare in Pakistan’s urban and peri-urban industrial zones. Vertix vertical-axis turbines operate in the turbulent, multi-directional wind found on rooftops and industrial estates. They have no directional requirement — they generate regardless of which direction the wind is coming from. Cut-in speed is 2–3 m/s, capturing energy in moderate wind conditions common across Karachi’s coastal belt, Lahore and Faisalabad’s industrial zones.

Power range 1–50 kW. Low noise output suitable for urban rooftop installation. Every Vertix deployment starts with a wind resource assessment — we model your site’s wind resource before recommending any turbine configuration.

Solar Systems

Tier-1 PV Integrated into Hybrid Architecture

Solar is the volume generation source in every hybrid system we design. How It Works

Four Layers. One Managed System.

A complete hybrid generation and storage system integrates four layers under a single energy management system. Each layer has a role. The EMS coordinates all four to minimise grid import, maximise self-consumption and maintain backup availability.

Solar
Daytime Generation

PV array generates during daylight hours. Primary generation source in most systems by volume.

Wind
Night & Overcast

Vertix generates when solar cannot — extending generation hours across 24-hour operational cycles.

BESS
Storage Buffer

Battery absorbs excess generation, smooths supply curves and discharges during peaks or outages.

EMS
Intelligent Control

Energy management system coordinates all sources — optimising dispatch against tariff, forecast and availability priority.
