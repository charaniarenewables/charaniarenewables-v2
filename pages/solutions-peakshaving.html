The Mechanism

How Peak Shaving Works

Your BESS charges during off-peak hours when grid power is cheap and demand is low. An energy management system monitors your real-time demand and discharges the battery when consumption approaches a set threshold — capping your maximum demand. The same battery provides backup during outages. One system, two functions, one business case.

Sizing a Peak Shaving System

Peak shaving system sizing requires analysis of your demand profile.

  • Obtain 12 months of AMI or smart meter data showing 15-minute demand intervals.
  • Identify peak demand periods and the demand threshold you wish to cap.
  • Calculate the energy (kWh) required to flatten demand to the target threshold.
  • Size BESS for peak shaving energy plus backup duration for critical loads.
  • Model savings against demand charge tariff to calculate payback period.
