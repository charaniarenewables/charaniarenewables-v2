LV vs HV

When choosing between low-voltage (LV) and high-voltage (HV) storage, consider load size, installation environment, and scalability. The key differences are highlighted below.

Low-Voltage (LV)

Suitable for residential and small commercial applications.

  • 48 V modules for safe installation
  • 5-15 kWh capacity range
  • Simple inverter integration (hybrid/solar)
  • Modular expansion for increased runtime

High-Voltage (HV)

Designed for commercial, industrial and utility-scale projects.

  • Rack-based 512 V systems
  • 50 kWh and above for peak shaving & backup
  • Scalable containerised options
  • Advanced EMS & integration with grid and renewables

ESS vs BESS

Energy Storage Systems (ESS) and Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) serve different scales. ESS refers to smaller modular units for homes, whereas BESS denotes industrial-scale solutions.

ESS - Energy Storage

Compact modules for domestic and light commercial use.

  • Typically under 20 kWh per unit
  • Backup for essential loads
  • Paired with hybrid inverters
  • Ideal for solar self-consumption

BESS - Battery Energy Storage

High-capacity systems for C&I and grid support.

  • 50 kWh to megawatt-hour scale
  • Peak shaving and demand management
  • Utility-grade EMS and monitoring
  • Integrates with wind, solar and grid infrastructure

Hybrid Compatibility

Proper storage selection hinges on compatibility with your generation mix and inverter platform. Our systems are designed to integrate seamlessly with solar, wind and grid sources.

Solar Pairing

LV ESS connects to hybrid inverters for self-consumption and backup. HV BESS integrates with commercial PV arrays via DC coupling or AC coupling.

Wind Pairing

Our storage platforms balance wind output fluctuations, storing excess energy and delivering smooth power to loads or the grid.

Inverter & Grid Integration

Compatibility with leading hybrid inverter manufacturers and grid-tie standards ensures safe operation. EMS controls manage charging, discharging and peak shaving.

Channel Guidance

Not sure which voltage class or system fits your site? Use these guidelines to start the conversation with our technical team or your installer.

Start with load: estimate your critical load and peak demand. LV is best for up to ~15 kWh daily consumption; HV is needed for larger industrial loads.

Installation environment: LV units are wall- or floor-mounted indoors. HV racks and containers require dedicated space and ventilation.

Scalability: choose LV modules for incremental growth or HV BESS for projects anticipating load expansion.

Consult our engineers: our team will run sizing simulations and design a system that meets your operational and financial goals.
