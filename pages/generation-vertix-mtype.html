Applications & Site Requirements

The M-Type Is a Horizontal-Axis Turbine — Site Matters

Unlike the Q-Type and GV-series vertical-axis turbines, the M-Type is a horizontal-axis wind turbine (HAWT). This means it yaws to face the wind and performs best on open sites with a consistent prevailing wind direction. It is not suited to turbulent rooftop environments or urban settings where wind changes direction frequently. For those applications, the Q-Type or GV-series VAWT is the correct choice.

Rural & Peri-Urban Sites

Open Terrain with Consistent Wind

The M-Type delivers best results on pole or tower mounts at open sites — farmland, rural facilities, coastal plots and peri-urban industrial land where prevailing wind is consistent and relatively unobstructed. At these sites it provides reliable 12/24V battery charging for lighting, pumping or security loads.

Off-Grid Battery Charging

Primary or Supplementary 12/24V Source

The M-Type outputs directly to 12V or 24V battery banks via a wind charge controller, the same interface as a solar charge controller. It can operate alongside a solar panel on the same battery bank, providing generation during hours when solar output is absent or reduced. Combined generation extends battery autonomy significantly.

Telecom & Remote Monitoring

Continuous Low-Load Power

At 300–500W rated output the M-Type is suited to continuous low-load applications — telecoms repeaters, remote sensors, monitoring stations and security cameras — particularly in coastal or open-country locations where wind is reliable. The electromagnetic braking system provides automatic overspeed protection without manual intervention.
