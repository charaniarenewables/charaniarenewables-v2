Choosing Your System

LV or HV — The Decision That Determines Everything

Voltage class is the first decision in any storage project. It determines the available products, the inverter architecture, the installation complexity and the total system cost. Get this wrong and the rest of the design is compromised.

Low Voltage (48V)

LV Systems — Residential and Small Commercial

48V architecture. Compatible with all major hybrid inverters in the Pakistani market — Growatt, Deye, Solis, SolarEdge and others. Capacity range 5–30 kWh. Simple installation, channel-partner delivered. Right for homes, small offices and light commercial loads.

Chemistry Matters

Why We Use Lithium Iron Phosphate

All Charania Renewables LV systems use LFP chemistry — not as a default, but because it is genuinely the right choice for Pakistan. LFP cells are thermally stable at the ambient temperatures common across Karachi, Lahore and Faisalabad. They do not enter thermal runaway under the heat stress that NMC cells are susceptible to in our climate. LFP delivers 3,000–6,000+ cycles at 80% depth of discharge versus 1,000–2,000 for NMC — a correctly sized LFP system will outlast the solar array it supports. Energy density is lower than NMC but entirely adequate for stationary backup and self-consumption. For a system that charges and discharges daily for 15–20 years in Pakistan, there is no better chemistry available at this price point.

We Size Before We Specify

The Right System Is Never a Catalogue Decision

The correct battery system depends on your daily consumption, backup duration requirement, peak demand profile, existing inverter capacity and budget. Too small and you run out of backup during extended outages. Too large and capital sits idle. We run a load analysis before recommending any product — LV or HV, MountRex, Aokly or Zetara. The product recommendation follows the analysis. Not the other way around.
