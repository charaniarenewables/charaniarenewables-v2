Generator Technology

Coreless Disc Generator

The GVH-5KW uses an axial flux coreless outer-rotor disc PMG — direct drive, no gearbox. Removing the iron core eliminates cogging torque and magnetic drag entirely, bringing start torque below 1 N·m. The turbine begins generating at wind speeds where conventional iron-core generators are still overcoming internal resistance. With no iron-core cogging there is no vibration at the source, and noise remains low across the operating range. The coreless structure delivers a power-to-weight ratio significantly higher than conventional motor-type alternators, with a design life 8× longer.

  • Start torque <1 N·m — generates in wind that stops conventional turbines
  • No iron-core cogging — vibration-free, low-noise operation
  • Patent-pending “Precise Coil” winding technology
  • SKF bearings throughout
  • 8× longer design life vs conventional motor-type generators
Solar Integration

Already Have Solar? The GVH-5KW Works With It.

Most commercial and industrial solar systems in Pakistan are under-generating for at least half of every 24-hour period — at night, in overcast conditions and during the morning and evening peaks when loads are highest but solar output is low. The GVH-5KW is designed to fill exactly that gap.

Off-Grid Systems

Add Wind to Your Battery Bank

The GVH-5KW connects to your existing battery bank via an MPPT wind charge controller — the same concept as your solar charge controller, but for wind. Your batteries now receive charge from two independent sources. When solar generation is zero, wind generation continues. The result is a more consistently charged battery bank and fewer hours running on generator.

On-Grid Systems

Feed Wind Into the Grid Alongside Solar

If your existing solar system is grid-tied, the GVH-5KW connects through an on-grid wind controller and grid-tie inverter, feeding generated units directly into your facility consumption at the same time as your solar system. Both sources offset grid import simultaneously. There is no conflict between the two — each operates on its own control path to the same inverter bus.

Hybrid Systems

Wind + Solar + BESS Together

The most complete integration: solar panels and the GVH-5KW both feed into a hybrid inverter (such as Deye or similar) alongside a BESS. The system charges batteries from whichever source is available, dispatches to load on priority, and exports surplus to grid. Wind at night charges the BESS for morning peak load. Solar during the day covers generation and tops up storage. The two sources are complementary by nature.

What You Need

Connecting the GVH-5KW to an existing solar system requires a wind charge controller or on-grid wind controller matched to the turbine output voltage, and in a hybrid setup, a hybrid inverter with a dedicated wind input or DC coupling.
